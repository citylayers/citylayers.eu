ABOUT CITY LAYERS

From Urban Challenges
To Everyday Experience

City Layers is a transdisciplinary framework that translates complex urban phenomena into accessible layers of everyday experience. Our citizen science methodology bridges community insights with expert knowledge, while our digital platform -the core of the project- enables intuitive participatory mapping and sophisticated data visualization. This transformative process converts subjective urban experiences into actionable intelligence, empowering citizens and decision-makers to collaboratively shape more responsive and equitable urban environments.

ACCOMPLISHMENTS AND EVENTS

Citizen Science Award 2023

City Layers was honoured with the Citizen Science Award 2023. Developed with over 300 citizens, students, and researchers, this participatory mapping tool enables users to share observations, add tags, and photos, and engage with others’ insights on urban spaces.

Responsive Cities 2023

Our participation in Responsive Cities 2023 featured the first iteration of City Layers—our innovative mapping framework and digital tool. We presented the methodology and initial participatory mapping results, demonstrating how citizen observations transform into collective spatial intelligence.

EU Researchers’ Night 2024

At European Researchers' Night in Graz, we showcased our collaborative mapping tool, empowering citizens to shape urban design through shared data. Visitors explored our responsive dashboard, which integrates community insights to create more resilient, inclusive cities.

Innovation Incubation Center 2024

City Layers has been accepted into the “Innovation Incubation Center” at TU Wien, recognizing our potential as a scalable, market-ready company. This program provides expert mentorship, business development resources, and industry connections, helping us refine our initiative and accelerate growth.

EU Sparks for Climate 2024

Our participation in “EU Sparks for Climate 2024” resulted in winning the Austrian and German regional competition! Our initiative, “City Layers for Greener Cities”, addressed the urgent need for urban green spaces. Our framework contributed valuable insights to help planners create more sustainable cities.

Maps of Collective Intelligence

How People’s Experiences Shape the Spatial Identity of the City

This map integrates six perceptual layers – Beauty, Sound, Movement, Protection, Climate Comfort, and Activities – based on how people experience the city. While the inner city scores high in beauty, protection, and walkability, it lacks in climate comfort and places to rest. The Donaukanal stands out for its natural soundscape and thermal comfort. Together, they form a human-driven map of urban quality, open to all

People-Driven Site Analyses

Lived experiences as a basis for spatial improvement

This zoomed-in analysis shows people’s perceptions of a specific site across comfort, enjoyability, rest, facilities, protection, and cleanliness. The map shows where these qualities – like areas with activity and comfort – are felt strongly. The insights help identify where small changes could make a big difference in how the space is used and enjoyed.
